The joke at work is often, “Microsoft designed WPF to fit two niches, photos, and RSS readers.”<\/p>\n

Not that there’s anything wrong with a photo printer. WPF seems to be good at “media”, less good at line of business applications. Bil discusses this very issue here<\/a>.<\/p>\n

I love DataTemplates in WPF. The rest — ah, isn’t as interesting to me anymore. Unless I needed the media features or DataTemplate feature, I wouldn’t use WPF for a line of business application anymore. I’d do web with a rich client (as needed) or WinForms.
