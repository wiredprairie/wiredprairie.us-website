There once was a day when I\u2019d go to a big box store shopping for electronics, cameras, whatever, and look at the boxes, and any posted information, and make a decision without consulting any other sources. Occasionally, I\u2019d talk to a friend or coworker, but more often than not, I shopped alone. There was no \u201cInternet Oracle\u201d that I could consult for thousands of reviews by other people that I don\u2019t know. I wouldn\u2019t spend months looking for a product, trying to decide, as there really wasn\u2019t much information besides what was on the box and what a \u201ccapable\u201d salesperson might be able to offer.<\/p>\n

Now, I\u2019m forced to look through hundreds of billions of web pages, reading and rereading reviews, going over every tiny detail and immediately moving on if someone has a bad review \u2013 even if there are 20 good reviews. Reviewer \u201cButtinski\u201d said it was the worst 2 speed blender and toaster combination!<\/p>\n

Am I happier now<\/strong>, with all of this information?<\/strong> Probably not, unless it\u2019s to just justify my educated purchase with a team of trusted internet co-shoppers.<\/p>\n

(Can you tell that I\u2019m shopping for something? Specifically a compact digital camera that fits in my front blue-jean pants pocket comfortably.
