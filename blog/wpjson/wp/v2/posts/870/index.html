What the Future Holds \u2013 Convergence of WPF and Silverlight<\/strong><\/h5>\n

 <\/p>\n

I recently spoke with Ian Ellison-Taylor at Microsoft. Ian is a General Manager at Microsoft, reporting directly to Scott Guthrie. Among other things, his group handles both Silverlight and WPF (and RIA services and a lot of other stuff). I figured if I wanted the skinny on the future, he\u2019d be the guy to talk to. So, he and I talked about the convergence of Silverlight and WPF, and later exchanged some email on the topic.<\/p>\n

 <\/p>\n

In the future, it is very likely that both Silverlight and WPF will be a single technology with a single codebase. After all, Silverlight was originally known as WPF\/E (E as in Everywhere), and in an amazing 180 degree reversal of our usual approach, we took an ugly codename and created a great product name (Silverlight) from it.<\/p>\n<\/blockquote>\n

D\u2019uh. Like it wasn\u2019t obvious, especially with the explosive growth and capabilities of Silverlight, why wouldn\u2019t this be on the road map? <\/p>\n

Of course, there\u2019s no time frame mentioned, so don\u2019t plan on your Silverlight application being able to use real 3D and actual DataTriggers any time soon.<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
