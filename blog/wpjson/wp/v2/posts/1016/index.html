Curious about text rendering characteristics between Flash 10, Silverlight 4.0 and Photoshop, I created two small applications, one in Flash CS 4, and one in Visual Studio 2010. The Silverlight 4.0 tests are using the release candidate which was current as of March 23, 2010.<\/p>\n

I used Times New Roman in all of the tests and used full text justification in all of the examples.<\/p>\n

There are two types of text rendering available in Silverlight, Animated and ClearType (the default).<\/p>\n

(You may click on images to see full size versions in a new window \u2013 I did that so that it would be easy for you to move the images around and do your own side-by-side comparisons). <\/p>\n

<\/a> <\/p>\n

Flash 10 has similar offerings, with an Anti-alias for readability and anti-alias for animation option:<\/p>\n

 <\/a> <\/p>\n

Side by side using the animated mode:<\/p>\n

<\/a> <\/p>\n

Then, the options for \u201creadability\u201d or the default compared side-by-side. (Note that I flipped the apps around so they\u2019d layer correctly below<\/em>)<\/p>\n

<\/a> <\/p>\n

To my eye, I preferred the Flash Anti-alias for Readability option just slightly over the other options. The animation friendly options left the text feeling too soft for me. What I didn\u2019t like about the Silverlight ClearType option was the darkness of the font overall.<\/p>\n

Zoomed in (with Silverlight on the top). You\u2019ll see how the Silverlight text uses darker strokes overall.<\/p>\n

I next tweaked the foreground color of the text in the Silverlight ClearType example, deciding that the black was too black for comfortable on screen reading.<\/p>\n

I set the foreground of the text to #3d3d3d (click image to enlarge).<\/p>\n

<\/a> <\/p>\n

Very close. So close that I\u2019m not sure that I could distinguish between the two renderings without detailed study. The lightening of the foreground color made the text look a bit softer than the original, but not so far as to make the text blurry. <\/p>\n

Below, you can see how the bowl of the letter \u2018a\u2019 in Flash 10 and the area along the meanline are nearly disconnected from the stem. This general lightness appears throughout Flash\u2019s rendering of Times New Roman. <\/p>\n
