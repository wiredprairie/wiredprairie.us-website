Why is that people feel that when asked a question like:<\/p>\n

\u201cHow do I convert my large MP3 files into a lower bit rate file so I can fit more songs on my smaller MP3 player?\u201d that they respond with far too often useless opinions:<\/p>\n

    \n
  • \u201cYou\u2019re destroying the quality of the music.\u201d <\/li>\n
  • \u201cGet a bigger music player\u201d <\/li>\n
  • \u201cJust sync less music to your player\u201d <\/li>\n
  • Etc., blah blah blah. <\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

    Many times, they\u2019re not even anonymous users or voices. What\u2019s worse is they rarely actually answer the question because they must honestly believe that their rationale is so sound that there\u2019s no need to continue address the original question, as they\u2019ve handled it sufficiently: \u201cYou are a moron for wanting this your way, do it my way.\u201d<\/p>\n

    It\u2019s like going to the store and looking for some orange juice. <\/p>\n

    \u201cWhich brand of orange juice should I buy?\u201d<\/p>\n

      \n
    • You shouldn\u2019t buy orange juice, instead buy oranges and make it fresh every day. Otherwise, your children will hate you and likely have scurvy, you slimy bastard. <\/li>\n
    • You don\u2019t want orange juice at all, you really should make your own pomegranate juice every day \u2013 it\u2019s full of blah blah blah. <\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

      Further annoyance \u2013 these aren\u2019t like big life decisions that we\u2019re talking about here where hearing out multiple opinions is terribly useful, certainly not to the degree that many take their arguments. <\/p>\n

      You\u2019re more than welcome to offer an opinion (as I\u2019m doing here), but consider tempering that with something that actually addresses the actual question. Maybe they don\u2019t have time to make fresh squeezed orange juice every morning?<\/p>\n

       <\/p>\n

      You see, I was looking for an answer to the MP3 problem above. I found far more useless dribble than I did actual help. Google may index zillions of web pages \u2013 but there are many that they should leave out. Arrgh.<\/em><\/p>\n

       <\/p>\n

      To answer the MP3 question above and what I\u2019ve tried, I\u2019ve tried a few different solutions all with different degrees of success. We have 53GB of MP3s right now \u2013 which is too large for my wife\u2019s iPod Touch. She wants most of the music on the player, so we reduce the bitrate and she is somewhat picky-and-choosy about the songs.<\/p>\n

      I\u2019m currently watching MediaMonkey<\/a> (a shareware app) convert the songs. It generally works, but I\u2019ve had to kill the process a few times as it\u2019s gone haywire on me (with errors that make no sense). The UI of MediaMonkey is terrible though overall \u2013 I really don\u2019t care for it (but we did pay for a lifetime subscription as it had a few features we found useful, but I wouldn\u2019t use it as my every day player).<\/p>\n

      We tried the MP3QualityModifier from here<\/a>, but it crashed and was also hard to get it to started with a large set of files.<\/p>\n
