Again, without seeing them, I wasn\u2019t sure of how\u2019d they feel. Would they fit in my pants pocket (without a carrying case)?<\/p>\n

  <\/p>\n

The only camera that seemed like it might fit would be the Panasonic. So, I compared it\u2019s size against my current favorite, the Canon Powershot 870<\/a>:<\/p>\n

<\/p>\n

(The Canon is on the left, Panasonic on right).<\/p>\n

So, the Panasonic would be a bit bigger, but it would probably fit in my jeans pocket. However, there were three things that kept me from jumping and buying the Panasonic:<\/p>\n

    \n
  • Lens cap<\/strong>. Like a DSLR, it has a lens cap that must be managed. There\u2019s a little \u201ctie\u201d of some sort that it ships with, but I like the convenience of not worrying about extra pieces when I\u2019m carrying a point and shoot camera.<\/li>\n
  • Zoom<\/strong>. A sad 2.5x zoom. I can\u2019t always use my feet to get me closer to things \u2013 so zoom comes in handy. 2.5 is really sad.<\/li>\n
  • Proprietary RAW. <\/strong>There\u2019s no indication that Adobe will support this custom RAW format any time soon. I like Lightroom 2.1 \u2026 I don\u2019t want to use more than one tool to process my images. <\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

    The image quality, from what I\u2019ve read is pretty good on the LX3. Better than a lot of cameras before it. The low light handling is also good (from what I\u2019ve read).<\/p>\n

    The Canon, although the G9 was well rated, is just too big and heavy for the point and shoot style camera I\u2019m looking for.<\/p>\n

    Then there was the Nikon \u2026 cool that it had built in GPS, (yet I read some people suggested it drained the battery too quickly and wasn\u2019t as accurate as other GPS chips \u2013 here for example<\/a>), it was a bit bigger than I would like. The RAW support, although more proprietary than the preview RAW formats released by Nikon cameras (NEF), is supported by Lightroom.<\/p>\n

    In the end, I decided not to buy any of these for now \u2013 and I\u2019d rather wait for something that doesn\u2019t have as many compromises for me. I bet they all will take great pictures \u2013 and if your needs are slightly different than mine, you\u2019d likely be very happy. (If you don\u2019t have a desire to carry the camera in a pants pocket\u2026.)<\/p>\n

    What did I do instead? I bought a new vibration reduction telephoto zoom lens for my D300! :) <\/p>\n

    I bought the Tamron 18-270mm f\/3.5-6.3 Di-II VC LD Asph. Macro Lens<\/a>. It\u2019s a 72mm 3.5-22\/6.3-40 f\/Stop lens with great wide-angle to awesome zoom abilities in a very compact lens. I own the 18-250 (no vibration reduction), which I\u2019ve taken many thousands of photos with and been extremely happy <\/strong>with the high quality images. So, I had no reason to doubt that this lens would be equally good for my needs. I could have bought the Nikon 18-200<\/a>, but the Tamron is a bit cheaper, has a better zoom range, and the brand has proven it\u2019s capable of good things. <\/p>\n

    Both lens are about the same length and weight \u2013 so I couldn\u2019t see any reason not to go with the slightly more capable lens. I\u2019m not a professional photographer and I don\u2019t have expectations that a $600 lens will be of the same quality as a $5000 lens (unlike some people in forums who seem to expect that). <\/p>\n

    I don\u2019t have any shots I can post yet from the Tamron 18-270mm, but when I do, I\u2019ll be sure to link to them (they\u2019ll be on my SmugMug page). <\/p>\n
